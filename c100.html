Background

The Commissioner's 100 is a growing movement of front-line, can-do Metropolitan Police officers and staff who want to improve the MPS from the bottom-up with ideas that have the potential and can be given the green light without becoming ensnared in never-ending red-tape that means they never the see the light of day.

The Commissioner embraced this group in July 2012 and attends all our events because he wants to meet front-line officers and hear their ideas for how we can make the MPS an even more effective organisation.

We have run five events so far, and numerous spin off projects have developed from the Commissioner's 100.

Technology and innovation

At the 4th Commissioner's 100, colleagues outlined a vision of just how policing could be through proper use of technology, and simple innovations that would save us immense amounts of time and money.

We considered the current provision of technology, which means that officers must spend a significant proportion of their time travelling to and from the police station between jobs - just to copy out information from their pocket notebooks into a computer system before they could get back to helping people.

The solutions we proposed are practical and will deliver staggering cost-savings over a very short period of time.

The hack day is all about demonstrating to all levels of command just how much can be achieved quickly and easily, without lengthy and costly painful procurement processes and bureaucracy.
