Suggestions

Ideas and starting points for your hack...

Source

We asked Metropolitan Police officers what ideas they could think of for apps to help them and the public to help keep London safe. Here's what they came up with!

Of course, some ideas are just too big to cover in a hack, and your own ideas and passion will help you to decide what you want to make.

Surprise us and show us what you can do!

Apps for police officers

  • Toolkits for officers reporting crimes from the scene.
  • Easily digestible guidance on relevant legislation.
  • Procedure handbooks (again emphasis on digestible).
  • Swatch guides: eg. a guide to the illegal fur trade.
  • Forms: save hours of wasted time copying out paperwork.
  • Mapping and routing: get to the scene of an incident quickly and safely.
  • Plan taskings: divide an area between officers on shift.
  • Coordinate a suspect/evidence search across a large area.
  • Describe and visualise the internal structure of a building:
    • ie. "there are 3 rooms on the 4th floor, it's the 2nd door on your left"
  • Communication aides: can you improve on radio solutions?
    • ie. a shared photo-stream between a team of officers.
  • Organisation of coverage for large events:
    • plan locations and times,
    • incorporate individual officers needs,
    • notify officers in advance and collect acknowledgements.
  • OCRing:
    • Photograph a number plate, OCR and put it in the clipboard.
    • Photograph any document or ID card, ask the user to indicate which part to OCR, and put that in the clipboard.
  • A shared briefing solution.
  • Local intelligence: augmented reality solutions to display geographic data and warnings.

Apps for the public

  • An app to help you secure your home.
  • A reminder app that tells you to lock your door as you go out.
  • Advice video library, eg. these.
  • An app to help you safely plan a night out:
    • Identifies the last tube home wherever you'll be.
    • Reminds you so you don't miss it!
    • Find you a registered mini cab service, or taxi rank.
    • Advice on what to do if your wallet or purse is stolen.
    • Advice on where to get help in an emergency.
  • What to look out for! Advice on hugger muggers and distraction theft.
  • An app for victims of burglary:
    • Offer victims reassurance.
    • Help them track their crime report.
    • Help them to contact and get to know their Safer Neighbourhood Team.
    • Advise them on what to do next, and what information to collect.
    • Advice on preserving the scene of the crime for evidence and prints.
  • Neighbourhood watch and community:
    • Help people connect with their Safer Neighbourhood Team.
    • Connect people in the same street - help them know each other.
    • A photostream for the neighbourhood watch.
  • Self-reporting: help us help people to self-reporting crimes.
  • Youngsters:
    • An app for members of the police cadets.
    • An app to help school kids communicate with their schools officer.
    • Help children report abuse and crime anonymously if they wish.
    • Help to build trust between youngsters, police and PCSOs.
